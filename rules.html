    Rule descriptions

    • All Wikipedia articles must be written from an impartial point of view. They should represent significant views fairly and without bias.
    • Wikipedia articles must not contain material such as facts, allegations, and ideas for which no reliable, published sources exist.
    • It might be tempting to work with a topic you’re directly involved in. But if you think you might have a Conflict Of Interest (COI), don’t create an article. Editors should never write articles about themselves or where they work.
    • This means that people reading and editing Wikipedia can check that any information comes from a reliable source.
    • Your sources should be published secondary or tertiary sources, as unpublished materials and interviews are not considered reliable. Your best bet is to use academic and peer-reviewed publications.
    • The topic must have “received significant coverage” or they should meet these specific guidelines for the topic category. However, just because something doesn’t pass these guidelines, doesn’t mean it’s not important. For example, the histories of black and brown people, queer and trans folk, etc. have often not received significant coverage.
    • Copying material without the permission of the copyright holder from sources that are not public domain or compatibly licensed is likely to be a copyright violation.
